Real-world Application of What We Will Learn

  • Product: PowerPot thermoelectric generator
  • Daymond: What do you want to do with the money? 3 things: Hire people, distribution network, expand the product line. How do you plan to raise money?
  • Module 1: Investment decisions and financing decisions
  • Kevin: Is there a patent, or can anybody do it?
  • Module 1: Tangible and intangible assets
  • What do sharks want as a shareholder? Mark: “I like the social aspects of it”
  • Module 1: Maximize shareholder value. But there are ethical considerations
  • Investors ask a lot of questions: Why do you need water? How long does it charge? How much did you raise on Kickstarter?
  • Module 2, 3 (Accounting): How to explain your business to investors? How to evaluate a business?
  • Daymond: “You asked for $250,000 for 10%. You value the company at $2.5 million?” Mark: “I’ll offer you $250,000 for 20%.” Deal: $250k for 12% share + 3% in advisory options
  • Module 4, 5: What are future cash flows worth today? How to value equity and bonds?
  • How did the entrepreneurs make those 3 investment decisions?
  • Module 6, 7 (Project Evaluation): How to evaluate projects and choose the best ones to pursue?
  • How do sharks choose the companies they want to invest in?
  • Module 8 (Risk-return Trade-off): How to choose the best companies to invest in?
  • Why do they give up their share? Can’t they get a bank loan?
  • Module 9 (Corporate Finance): How to choose between debt and equity?
